Professional profile

A musician-scholar with classroom range, university depth, and production fluency.

John Sokar is a music educator and flutist based in North Carolina. His work spans elementary general music, applied flute instruction, ensemble leadership, curriculum development, music technology, live sound, and cross-cultural pedagogy.

He teaches music as a disciplined craft and a human language: structured, creative, reflective, and accessible to students from many backgrounds.

Current workMusic Teacher with Wake County Public School System, including Jones Dairy Elementary School and Reedy Creek Elementary School.
Higher educationFormer Assistant Professor and University Lecturer at Helwan University, teaching flute performance and leading woodwind work.
Applied artistryFlute pedagogy, performance preparation, tone production, stylistic awareness, and expressive performance.
Creative technologyLogic Pro X, Ableton Live, Cakewalk Sonar, Sibelius, MuseScore, audio systems, recording, and sound engineering.

Experience arc

John's career combines public school teaching, university-level instruction, performance leadership, music and arts coordination, and technical production support.

2023 - Present

Music Teacher, Jones Dairy Elementary School

Teaches general music, leads performances, integrates Orff-based instruction, and supports school-wide arts initiatives within Wake County Public School System.

2015 - 2023

Music Teacher, Durham and Wake County Schools

Delivered comprehensive music instruction, directed productions and concerts, supported arts integration, and brought creative tools into the classroom.

Music and Arts Coordinator, Private German School Cairo

Expanded curriculum and resources, directed choir, band, Orff ensembles, school events, acoustic improvements, technical support, and design materials.

1995 - 2001

University Lecturer and Orchestral Flutist

Taught flute at Helwan University and performed as a flute player with Cairo Opera House Orchestra productions.
